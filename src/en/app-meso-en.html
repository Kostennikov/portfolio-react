MESO — an app for ordering goods from local srocery stores

MESO is an app for ordering delivery from local grocery stores. I’ve designed the first version of the app.

Resource saving manifesto

All apps serve purposes beyond the boundaries of the interface. When customers use a delivery app, they don’t want to place an order, they want food already laying on their table and preferably cooked. The app is a technical layer on a way to enjoying delicious food. The more invisible and tiny this layer is, the better the attitude towards the app and therefore to the service.

At the same time, it’s important to consider development resources and don’t fall into perfectionism. Good design is a balance between care about users and business objectives.

Store selection

The first step is store selection. We made a map, so that users could immediately estimate the number of stores and select those, that they already know. Stores are divided into categories, that can be filtered.

There is a minimalistic catalogue with icons inside each store, and just as minimalistic view of goods and shopping cart. Not a single unnecessary line or step.

Returns

If a user is dissatisfied with the order, it makes no sense to design dark patterns that prevent return of goods — it will ruin the relationship. Good interface of return can make level of dissatisfaction much lower and save the relationship, because one mistake is not critical compared to a spiteful attitude.

Work on design was done in multiple steps in between
2018 and 2019. Developed without design control.
