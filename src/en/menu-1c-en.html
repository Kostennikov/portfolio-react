Major renewal of
1C platform menu
Problems

1C menu contains links to every part of the application, and there are a lot of them. On one hand it’s a good solution for large companies, because there's no need to customize the menu for different groups of users. On the other — most of the links are needless. Accountants, for example, are forced to look for the right links among hundreds of others and adaptation becomes very long and tedious. A setting allows to hide unnecessary links, but only a few users know about it.

The menu looked sloppy with only a few links.

Column widths were different in each section and made it difficult to target links.

All of this and other problems were solved

The menu tightened and became slender, stopped blocking the entire screen with emptiness. The width of collumns became fixed in each section independently of text length. Columns were formed by the "more on the left" rule but with a matter of habit: what was on the right stayed on the right.

The easy way

The main feauture — link highlights that are used oftenly. 1C logs actions and knows about all the user's transitions, so the redisign, although it brakes some habits, compensates for it with explicit hints.

Neo Superfunction

Turns out that some users keep tasks in "Favourites". I was pleasantly surprised by it and suggested refinements to level up personal micromanagement. This way grouping and text tags with emojis were born.

During the process it became clear that emojis is enough to recognize a group of tasks, so we omitted the text part. When the emojis are similar the text stays.
