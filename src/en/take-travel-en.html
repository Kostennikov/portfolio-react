Take Travel — is a website for bying tours, excursions and all sorts of different tickets. Previously, there was a separate website for each country, but this put spokes in the wheels of the scaling machine. My task was to collect everything in one place and make some room for experiments.

I called Sasha Radionov and he found a clear and steady metaphor in road signs. Travel — is the path from desire to impression, and Take Travel — is navigation on this path.

Globally, site users are divided into two groups: the first know what they want and the second just want something.
take.travel
Still a work in progress

For the first group of users it's important not to be interfered with searching what they came for. The site, seeing their search queries, prepares a lot of filtered pages that maintain identity in any language.

Filters help to leave only interesting things and shorten the way to making a purchase, but more about it later.

For the second group it's important not to get strained or confused during the search.
The site determines their location and suggests activities to fill their travel with.

The site rarely makes mistakes in determining the location, so it doesn't ask unnecessary clarifying question. But still, in case of an error, there's a way to choose a place.

If for some reason the location is not determined, users get to the main page, on top of which there are road signs indicating directions. And on the right there's a filter that helps you quickly find a place and activities.

This page is exceptionally nice on Desktop.

An additional way to take the load off the user — is to take it on yourself. To do this «Random Events» were made, that are not entirely random. Previously set filters are saved here, but if necessary, they can be quickly turned off. And mundane things like transfers do not initially fall into the selection.

Almost accidental design

Paths of different users intersect on the event pages.

The description of the services is similar to an article with vivid quotes. This dilutes the monotomy and allows you not to read the entire text. You can scroll through, stopping at interesting parts of the text. A well-written text directs attention and saves time.

In cases where you need to focus on the parameters of the service or there are simply no good photos, an alternative header design is used, unpretentious to the quality and the quantity of the photos.

The next step after the filters — is opening the service page. For example, transfers show information about the car model, capacity and travel time. Those who value comfort will not spend much time making sure the service doesn't meet the spirit of hedonism.

A wide selection of events implies a large amount of information necessary for ordering. In some cases, it's enough to specify the day and number of tickets, and in others you may need the date, time, quantity, address for the transfer and passport data. And it's important that the necessary minimum is requred, because filling out forms is not so much fun.

We have it all done just right in Take Travel :-)

Most of the users are already traveling and using local SIM cards, saving every byte. Therefore, there are no unnecessary photos and decorations on the site. Just like on road signs.

Many more pages were designed but it's time to wrap it up.
